# Key Metrics

## Detailed Insights
### Customer Preference by RAM

- The analysis shows that all RAM configurations such as 4GB, 6GB, 16GB, and 32GB contribute almost equally, each accounting for approximately 16 to 17 percent of total demand. This indicates that customer demand is evenly distributed across different configurations, and the business should maintain a balanced inventory strategy rather than focusing on a single variant.

### Customer Distribution by Region

- The regional distribution of customers is highly balanced, with each region contributing approximately 19 to 21 percent of total customers. This suggests that the business has a strong and consistent presence across all regions without reliance on a specific geographic area.

### Customer Distribution by Brand

- The analysis of brand preference shows that major brands such as Google, Apple, Samsung, and Nokia hold nearly equal shares, ranging between 48 to 52 percent. This indicates a highly competitive market environment where no single brand dominates, and purchasing decisions are likely influenced by pricing, features, and availability rather than brand loyalty alone.

### Product Preference

- The distribution between laptops and mobile phones is nearly equal, with each category contributing approximately 50 percent of total demand. This reflects a balanced product portfolio and suggests that the company should continue investing in both product categories equally.

### Customer-Level Purchase Behavior

- The data highlights that individual customer preferences vary significantly, with some customers showing a strong inclination toward laptops while others prefer mobile phones. This presents an opportunity to implement personalized marketing strategies and recommendation systems to improve customer engagement and sales.

# Detailed Insights
### Sales by Region

- Each region contributes approximately 1000 million or more in sales, indicating a stable and well-distributed revenue stream across all geographic areas. This reinforces the observation of balanced market penetration.

### Quantity Sold by RAM

- The quantity sold across different RAM configurations is almost equal, with each segment contributing around 45,000 to 46,000 units. This further confirms that customer demand is evenly spread across product variants.

### Total Sales by Product Category

- The contribution of laptops and mobile phones to total sales is almost identical, with laptops accounting for approximately 50.1 percent and mobile phones contributing 49.9 percent. This highlights the importance of maintaining a dual-product strategy.

### Brand-wise Quantity Sold

- The quantity sold for top brands such as Google, Nokia, Apple, and Samsung is nearly the same, with each brand contributing approximately 14,000 units. This indicates a highly competitive environment with minimal variation in sales volume among brands.

### Brand-wise Revenue Analysis

- Although the quantity sold across brands is similar, there are variations in revenue generation. For instance, Google generates the highest revenue among all brands, suggesting that it operates in a higher price segment or offers premium products.

### Storage Capacity and Sales Trend

- Products with higher storage capacities, such as 2TB and other premium configurations, contribute significantly more to total revenue compared to lower storage variants. This indicates that customers are inclined toward higher-value products, making premium offerings a key driver of revenue.

## Detailed Insights
### Year-wise Sales Distribution

- The year 2024 contributes approximately 50 percent of total sales, making it the highest-performing year. The year 2023 contributes around 35 percent, while 2025 contributes only 15 percent. This indicates a peak in performance during 2024 followed by a noticeable decline in 2025.

## Customer Distribution by Year

- The distribution of customers across years follows a similar pattern to sales, with the highest number of customers recorded in 2024. This suggests that the increase in sales during 2024 was driven primarily by higher customer acquisition.

### Monthly Sales Trend

- The monthly sales trend remains relatively stable, with average sales ranging between 200 million and 220 million. The absence of significant spikes indicates consistent business performance but also suggests limited seasonal or promotional impact.

### RAM-wise Sales Trend Over Time

- Higher RAM and storage configurations, along with the "Others" category, consistently generate higher revenue across different time periods. This reinforces the importance of premium product segments in driving overall profitability.

## Overall Business Insights
### Strengths

- The business demonstrates balanced performance across regions, products, and brands. There is consistent demand across different configurations, and the company maintains a stable revenue stream without dependency on a single segment.

### Challenges

- The analysis reveals a lack of strong growth trends, particularly with declining performance in 2025. The market is highly competitive, with minimal differentiation between brands. Additionally, the evenly distributed demand suggests that the company lacks a dominant product or strategy.

## Recommendations
### Business Strategy

- The company should focus on expanding its premium product offerings, particularly in high RAM and high storage categories, as these generate higher revenue. Efforts should also be made to improve marketing strategies during low-performing periods, especially in 2025.

### Customer Strategy

- Implementing customer segmentation and personalized recommendation systems can help improve customer engagement and increase conversion rates.

### Revenue Strategy

- The company should prioritize high-margin brands and introduce bundled offers to increase the average order value and overall profitability.
